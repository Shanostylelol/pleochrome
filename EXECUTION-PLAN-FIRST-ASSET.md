## PHASE 1: ASSET INTAKE (The $10M Emerald Barrel)

### 1.1 Asset Holder Intake
- [ ] **Have the asset holder complete the Intake Questionnaire**
  - Template is in: PleoChrome-Drive/00 - Templates and Trackers/Asset Holder Intake Questionnaire.md
  - Collect: Their identity, the barrel details, how they acquired it, existing certifications, photos
  - **WHAT TO ASK:** "Tell us everything about these emeralds — where they came from, how you got them, what paperwork you have, and what you think they're worth."

### 1.2 KYC/KYB on the Asset Holder
- [ ] **Verify their identity**
  - If individual: Government ID + liveness check
  - If company: Formation documents + identify all owners >25%
  - Use: Brickken's built-in KYC (150 included), or Veriff at $0.80/check, or Didit (free)
  - **WHAT THIS MEANS:** We're confirming they are who they say they are. Like a bank checking your ID before opening an account.

### 1.3 Sanctions & PEP Screening
- [ ] **Screen against OFAC SDN list** at sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov (free)
- [ ] **Screen against EU/UN sanctions** at opensanctions.org (free for non-commercial)
- [ ] **Check for PEP status** (Politically Exposed Person — politicians, diplomats, their families)
  - **WHAT THIS MEANS:** Making sure this person isn't on any government "do not do business with" list. If they are, we STOP immediately.
  - **HOW:** Enter their full name at the OFAC website. Takes 30 seconds. Document the result (even "no results found").

### 1.4 Provenance Review
- [ ] **Trace the chain of custody** for these emeralds
  - Where were they mined? (Country, mine name if known)
  - Who has owned them? (Every hand they've passed through)
  - Do they have import/export documentation?
  - Are they from a conflict zone? (Check OECD guidance)
  - **WHAT THIS MEANS:** Like a CARFAX for gemstones. We need to know the full history. Any gap = a red flag.

### 1.5 Review Existing Documentation
- [ ] **Catalog what the asset holder already has:**
  - GIA report(s) — if yes, verify at gia.edu/report-check-landing
  - Previous appraisals — from whom? When? What methodology?
  - Purchase receipts or invoices
  - Insurance records
  - Photos/video
  - **CRITICAL:** A "barrel of emeralds" is different from a single stone. We need to understand: How many stones? What quality range? Are they loose or mounted? What's the breakdown of value?

### 1.6 Execute the Intake Agreement
- [ ] **Have securities counsel draft the engagement agreement**
  - Defines: PleoChrome's fees (2% setup, 1.5% success, 0.75% annual admin)
  - Defines: Asset holder's obligations (provide documentation, pay pass-through costs, cooperate with appraisals)
  - Defines: Timeline expectations, gate conditions, termination provisions
  - Both parties sign electronically
  - **WHAT THIS MEANS:** This is the contract that governs the entire relationship. Nothing moves forward without it.

## PHASE 2: CERTIFICATION & VALUATION

### 2.1 GIA Certification
- [ ] **If the barrel already has GIA reports:** Verify each at gia.edu/report-check-landing
- [ ] **If not:** Submit to GIA for grading
  - For a barrel of multiple stones, each significant stone needs its own report
  - GIA fees: $150-$500 per stone depending on size
  - Turnaround: 2-3 weeks
  - **WHAT THIS MEANS:** GIA is like the DMV for gemstones — they give each stone an official ID card with its exact specifications.

### 2.2 SSEF Origin Determination (if needed)
- [ ] **For high-value colored stones, origin matters enormously**
  - Colombian emeralds command premium pricing
  - SSEF uses spectroscopy to determine geographic origin
  - Cost: CHF 4,000 (~$4,500) per stone
  - **DECISION:** For a $10M barrel, we may not need individual SSEF reports on every stone. Discuss with appraisers.

### 2.3 Select 3 Independent Appraisers
- [ ] **Interview the 3 appraiser candidates** (see Appraiser Interview Checklist below)
- [ ] **Verify credentials:**
  - CGA (Certified Gemologist Appraiser) or MGA (Master Gemologist Appraiser)?
  - USPAP training current?
  - Years of experience with emeralds specifically?
  - No affiliation with the asset holder, PleoChrome, or each other?
- [ ] **Assign sequentially** — Appraiser 1 goes first, ships to Appraiser 2, then 3
  - Each works independently, doesn't know the others' findings
  - Each writes a USPAP-compliant written report
  - **BARREL NOTE:** For a barrel of multiple stones, the appraisers value the collection as a whole AND the key individual pieces.

### 2.4 Run the 3-Appraisal Rule
- [ ] **Collect all 3 reports**
- [ ] **Calculate variance** — if any two appraisals differ by more than 15-20%, flag for review
- [ ] **Average the two lowest** — this is the offering value
- [ ] **Document the reconciliation** in a formal Variance Analysis Report
  - Example: Appraisals come in at $9.5M, $10.2M, $9.8M → Average of two lowest = ($9.5M + $9.8M) / 2 = $9.65M offering value
  - **WHAT THIS MEANS:** We always price conservatively. The asset holder might be disappointed, but every investor is protected.
