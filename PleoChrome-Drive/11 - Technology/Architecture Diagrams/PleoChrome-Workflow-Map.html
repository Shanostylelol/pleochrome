01
Acquisition
Building the Pipeline

Source high-value stones from vaults, dealer networks, and direct holders. Verify seller identity, negotiate terms, execute intake agreements.

1.1
Source Identification
RWDGAI
Three channels: (A) Already-vaulted stones at Brink's/Malca-Amit with existing GIA reports. (B) Dealer networks, auction houses (Christie's, Sotheby's), and mining consortiums. (C) Direct holder submissions via PleoChrome intake portal. AI can score and rank pipeline opportunities by documentation completeness and estimated value.
1.2
Initial Screening
DGAI
Quick-kill assessment: Does the stone meet minimum value thresholds ($100K+)? Does initial provenance documentation exist? Is the seller/holder in a non-sanctioned jurisdiction? Is the stone type suitable for tokenization (emeralds, rubies, sapphires, high-value diamonds)? Reject early, save everyone time.
1.3
Asset Holder Intake
DGRW
Seller completes full intake questionnaire: personal/entity identity, stone specifications (type, carat, color, origin claim), acquisition history, provenance chain, existing certifications (GIA reports, prior appraisals), high-resolution photos (min 4 angles + scale), and seller representations (clear title, no liens, not previously tokenized).
1.4
KYC / KYB on Seller
PTDGAI
Full identity verification via Sumsub or ComPilot: government ID with liveness check (individuals), formation documents + UBO identification for >25% owners (entities). Source of wealth verification. Background screening. Enhanced due diligence for high-risk profiles (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions).
1.5
Sanctions & PEP Screening
PTAI
Screen seller + all beneficial owners against OFAC SDN list, EU/UN consolidated sanctions, PEP databases, and adverse media. Blockchain wallet screening via Chainalysis if crypto-connected. Any hit = escalate to compliance officer. Document all screening results.
1.6
Provenance & Title Review
LGRWAI
Verify complete chain of custody from mine to current holder. Confirm clear legal title — no liens, encumbrances, or competing claims. Verify stone has not been previously tokenized, securitized, or pledged as collateral. OECD Due Diligence Guidance compliance. Kimberley Process (diamonds). AI can parse and cross-reference provenance documents.
1.7
Price Negotiation
RW
Negotiate tokenization terms with asset holder: PleoChrome fee structure (setup fee $5K-$50K + success fee 0.5-3% + annual admin 0.5-1%), pass-through costs (3 appraisals, vault storage, insurance, legal docs — paid by asset holder), and expected offering value range. All costs transparent and documented.
1.8
Intake Agreement
LGRW
Execute master engagement agreement between PleoChrome and asset holder. Defines: obligations of both parties, fee structure, timeline expectations, conditions for proceeding through all 7 gates, termination provisions, and confidentiality. Signed electronically via OneSpan Sign (WORM-compliant).

02
Preparation
Building the Evidence Layer

Transform a raw stone into a fully documented, independently valued, institutionally vaulted, and legally structured asset. Every claim verified by independent specialists.

2.1
GIA Lab Submission
PTRW
Ship stone to GIA (or SSEF/Gubelin for colored stones). Lab performs: cut, color, clarity, carat weight grading. For colored stones: geographic origin determination via spectroscopic analysis and treatment disclosure (heat, irradiation, filling). Turnaround: 2-3 weeks. GIA report is the industry gold standard — non-negotiable.
2.2
Report Verification
DGAI
Verify GIA report authenticity via GIA Report Check system (online verification using report number + carat weight). Confirm stone identity matches submitted documentation. Cross-reference any pre-existing reports. AI can flag discrepancies between seller claims and lab results.
2.3
3x Independent Appraisals
PTRW
Ship stone sequentially to 3 independent appraisers from approved panel. Each must be CGA/MGA certified, USPAP-compliant, and have zero affiliation with seller, PleoChrome, or each other. Each performs physical inspection + formal written appraisal. 5-10 business days each. Insured inland marine transit between appraisers. Cost: $100-$5,000+ per appraisal depending on stone value. Total phase: 3-4 weeks.
2.4
Valuation Reconciliation
DGAI
Analyze variance across 3 appraisals. If spread >15-20%, flag for 4th appraisal or reject. The 3-Appraisal Rule: Average the two lowest values to determine structurally conservative offering baseline. Compare to market comparables (recent auction records at Christie's/Sotheby's, dealer wholesale prices). AI can automate comparable analysis.
2.5
Vault Intake & Custody
PTRW
Transport stone to Brink's or Malca-Amit via insured specialized carrier. Vault intake: verify stone against GIA report, photograph, assign segregated storage location (not commingled), activate climate control. Issue Vault Receipt / Custody Acknowledgment. Malca-Amit offers Free Trade Zone facilities (100% duty-free). Activate API reporting feed for PleoChrome system integration.
2.6
Insurance Verification
RWDG
Verify vault insurance covers this specific asset at appraised value. Confirm no coverage gaps between transport, storage, and any future movement. Lloyd's of London is the gold standard (used by Tiamonds). Document policy numbers, coverage limits, sublimits for gemstones, and expiration dates. Set renewal tracking.
2.7
Chainlink PoR Setup
PTDG
Build custom External Adapter connecting to vault's inventory API. Deploy Proof of Reserve feed contract on Polygon. Define update triggers: custody change events trigger immediate update, plus daily attestation. Multiple independent Chainlink nodes verify and aggregate. On-chain feed reports: # stones held, appraised value, custody status. Smart contracts query this before any mint. Reference: Serenity Labs (Feb 2026) built similar for precious metals with Chainlink + Zoniqx.
2.8
SPV Formation
LG
Create new Series under Delaware Series LLC master entity (PleoChrome Holdings LLC). File with DE Division of Corporations ($110). Each series is bankruptcy-remote — one stone's issues don't affect others. Draft Operating Agreement defining: token holder economic rights, distribution waterfall, governance, transfer restrictions, dissolution. Obtain EIN. Open SPV bank account.
2.9
Legal Document Suite
LG
Securities attorney drafts: PPM (Private Placement Memorandum — risk factors, use of proceeds, management, tax), Subscription Agreement (investor representations, accreditation self-cert, payment terms), Token Purchase Agreement (links on-chain token to legal rights in SPV). Internal compliance review of all documents. Cost: $15K-$50K per offering. Templates reduce cost for subsequent stones.
2.10
Photo & Media Documentation
RWAI
Professional photography under standardized lighting (minimum 8 angles + macro detail + scale reference + 360 video). Independent weight verification on calibrated scale. Create marketing-ready assets for investor materials, data room, and platform listing. All media timestamped and hashed as part of evidence trail.

03
Tokenization
Minting the Digital Asset

Deploy a compliant ERC-3643 security token on Polygon via Brickken. Oracle-gated minting ensures nothing is created without verified reserves.

3.1
Token Configuration
DGPT
Configure in Brickken API: token name, symbol, total supply (offering value / min investment), decimals. Compliance rules: KYC whitelist required, accredited investor status required, jurisdiction blocks (sanctioned countries), max holder limit, lock-up period, transfer restrictions. Trusted issuers for ONCHAINID identity claims. Agent addresses for admin operations.
3.2
Testnet Deployment
DGPT
Deploy ERC-3643 token contract + escrow contract on Polygon testnet via Brickken sandbox (dapp.sandbox.brickken.com). Deploy ONCHAINID identity infrastructure (ClaimTopicsRegistry, TrustedIssuersRegistry, IdentityRegistry). Attach hashed legal documents on-chain. Configure mint-gate to require Chainlink PoR verification before any token creation.
3.3
Integration Testing
DGAI
Full lifecycle testing: (1) Mint tokens to whitelisted wallet — should succeed. (2) Transfer between whitelisted wallets — should succeed. (3) Transfer to non-whitelisted wallet — should revert. (4) Change PoR custody status to false — minting should halt. (5) Verify compliance rules block non-accredited, wrong jurisdiction, over-limit. Automated test suite recommended.
3.4
Smart Contract Audit
PT
Independent security audit by CertiK, Hacken, QuillAudits, or OpenZeppelin ($5K-$30K). Verify: no vulnerabilities, no backdoors, no logic errors, no infinite mint vectors, no reentrancy. Audit report filed as permanent evidence. Required for institutional credibility. Each unique compliance module should be audited independently.
3.5
Mainnet Deployment
DG
Deploy audited contracts to Polygon mainnet. Verify ALL parameters match testnet exactly. Confirm PoR feed connected and reporting correctly on mainnet (not testnet feed). Record contract addresses permanently in PleoChrome evidence system. This is irreversible — double-check everything.
3.6
Regulatory Filings
LG
File Form D with SEC via EDGAR (required within 15 days of first sale — no filing fee). File blue sky notices in each state where investors will be solicited (fees vary by state). Formally engage broker-dealer for compliant distribution. Prepare marketing materials — must include proper disclaimers for Reg D 506(c).
G6
PLATFORM GATE
Token live, PoR verified, audited, parameters match legal docs
04
Distribution
Token Sale & Ongoing Management

Execute a controlled Reg D 506(c) offering to qualified investors. Every buyer verified, every transaction recorded, evidence trail continues indefinitely.

4.1
Investor Outreach
PTRW
Targeted outreach via broker-dealer network. Reg D 506(c) permits general solicitation (websites, social media, events, advertising) — unlike 506(b). All investors MUST be verified accredited. Marketing materials must be compliant (include risk disclosures, "for accredited investors only" language). Broker-dealer handles distribution compliance.
4.2
Investor KYC & Accreditation
PTDGAI
Each investor completes: gov ID + liveness check, address verification, accredited investor verification. 2025 SEC simplification: investments of $200K+ (individuals) or $1M+ (entities) qualify for self-certification — no third-party verification needed. Below those thresholds: tax returns, bank statements, CPA/attorney letter, or broker confirmation. Sanctions screening on every investor. Via VerifyInvestor.com or ComPilot.
4.3
Subscription Execution
LGDG
Investor reviews PPM (Private Placement Memorandum), signs Subscription Agreement + Token Purchase Agreement electronically. Wire funds to SPV escrow account (funds never touch PleoChrome — goes directly to the SPV). Broker-dealer reviews and formally accepts the subscription. All executed via WORM-compliant e-signature.
4.4
Wallet Whitelist & Mint
DGPT
Add verified investor's wallet address to ERC-3643 Identity Registry (ONCHAINID). PoR gate confirms custody status = green. Chainlink Secure Mint: contract checks reserves >= supply + requested mint. If yes → tokens minted proportional to investment. If no → transaction reverts. Transfer tokens to investor wallet. Update cap table in PleoChrome system.
4.5
Investor Confirmation
DG
Send investor confirmation package: token quantity, wallet address, contract address, cap table position, Polygon block explorer link, access credentials for investor portal/data room, ongoing reporting schedule (quarterly + annual), and PleoChrome support contact.
4.6
Ongoing: PoR Monitoring
DGPTAI
Chainlink PoR feed runs continuously (24/7). Daily attestation confirms stone remains in vault. Any custody change triggers automated alert to PleoChrome operations. Token minting auto-halts if reserves < supply. Chainlink Automation can trigger circuit breakers — halting all minting, burning, and transfers when reserve thresholds are not met. Dashboard monitors all active assets.
4.7
Ongoing: Reporting
DGAI
Quarterly: custody confirmation, PoR status, market comparable updates, any material events. Annually: independent re-appraisal by new appraiser, K-1 tax statements for all investors, insurance renewal verification, Form D amendment filing. AI can automate report generation from structured data.
4.8
Ongoing: Compliance
LGAI
Re-screen all investors quarterly against OFAC/sanctions lists. Monitor for accreditation status changes. File SARs when suspicious activity triggers are met. Annual independent AML program audit by external firm. Multi-jurisdiction compliance tracking (US Reg D, EU MiCA, UAE VARA, Singapore MAS). AI-powered transaction monitoring via ComPilot or Chainalysis.
4.9
Secondary Transfers
DG
Token holder initiates transfer. ERC-3643 compliance module automatically verifies: recipient wallet is in Identity Registry (KYC verified), recipient has accredited investor claim, recipient jurisdiction is not blocked, transfer doesn't exceed max holder limit, lock-up period has expired. Compliant = transfer executes on-chain. Non-compliant = blocked at smart contract level. No manual intervention needed. Secondary trading possible via tZERO ATS (24/7, SEC/FINRA regulated).
4.10
Redemption
RWLGDG
If a holder (or group holding 100% of tokens) wants the physical stone: (1) Submit redemption request through PleoChrome portal. (2) Burn all tokens for that series (maintaining supply/reserve parity). (3) Coordinate vault release with custodian. (4) Arrange insured specialized transport to holder's registered address. (5) Delivery confirmation + ownership transfer documentation. (6) SPV series dissolves. (7) Full audit trail preserved permanently. Note: Redemption logistics are an underserved market — major software opportunity.

SALE GATE
