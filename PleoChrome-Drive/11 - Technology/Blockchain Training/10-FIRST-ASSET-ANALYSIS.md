## CRITICAL OBSERVATIONS FOR PLEOCHROME

### 1. The Appraisal Is 3 Years Old
- Dated April 25, 2023. It's now March 2026.
- **For tokenization, we need fresh independent appraisals.** This one can serve as a reference but cannot be the basis for an offering.
- The 3-Appraisal Rule requires 3 NEW independent appraisals, none of which can be from the same appraiser.

### 2. This Is ROUGH/UNCUT Emerald — Not Polished
- Rough emerald has a completely different market than polished/cut emeralds
- The $100.65/carat value is for ROUGH material in matrix (still embedded in host rock)
- The photos show emerald crystals embedded in quartz/mica matrix — typical rough
- **Rough vs polished pricing is dramatically different.** Rough trades at a fraction of polished value. The appraisal methodology must account for this.

### 3. Valuation Methodology Needs Scrutiny
- The appraisal values ALL material at $102.83/carat uniformly
- In reality, rough emerald quality varies enormously within a barrel — some pieces are gem-quality, others are specimen-grade, others are essentially matrix rock with emerald inclusions
- **For PleoChrome's 3-Appraisal Rule, we need appraisers who specifically grade piece-by-piece or at minimum grade by quality tier within the barrel**

### 4. The Chain of Custody Has Multiple Entities
- Originally appraised for: **Gem Meta Vault, LLC** (Las Vegas, Wyoming filing)
- Vault receipt in name of: **White Oak Partners, II, LLC / Moneytronix**
- Agreement between: **Moneytronix** and **White Oak/ARC**
- The barrels were originally in **Sarasota, Florida** (Sarasota Vault Depository)
- Now in **University Place, Washington** (Olympic Vault)
- **PleoChrome needs to trace the complete chain of custody:** Who owned what, when, and how did it transfer from Gem Meta Vault to White Oak to its current status?

### 5. Olympic Vault Is NOT Brink's or Malca-Amit
