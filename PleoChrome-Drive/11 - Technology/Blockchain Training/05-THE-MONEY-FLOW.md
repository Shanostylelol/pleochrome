# Module 5: How the Money Actually Flows (Follow Every Dollar)

## The One-Sentence Version
In the tokenization process, real-world dollars flow through a very specific sequence, and understanding who pays whom, when, and why is the difference between sounding like an amateur and sounding like a pro.

---

## The Complete Money Flow (A $10M Emerald Example)

Let's trace every dollar through the entire PleoChrome process using the $10M emerald barrel as our example.

### STARTING POINT

**Stone owner has:** A barrel of certified emeralds they believe is worth $10 million.

**PleoChrome has:** The platform, the process, and the partner network.

**Investors have:** Cash they want to invest in alternative assets.

The job of this process is to connect these three groups in a way that's compliant, verified, and profitable for everyone.

---

### MONEY EVENT 1: The Setup Fee (Day 1)

**What happens:** The stone owner signs the engagement agreement and pays PleoChrome the setup fee.

```
Stone Owner → $200,000 → PleoChrome
                         (2% of $10M claimed value)
```

**When:** At engagement signing, before ANY work begins.

**Why:** This covers PleoChrome's costs of coordinating the entire pipeline (KYC, legal, technology integration, project management). It also demonstrates the stone owner's commitment — if they're not willing to pay the setup fee, they're not serious.

**What PleoChrome does with this money:**
- Pays for KYC/KYB verification ($50-$500)
- Pays securities counsel for PPM ($7,500-$12,000)
- Pays for Brickken platform ($5,500)
- Pays for Chainlink integration ($2,500)
- Pays for smart contract config review ($3,500)
- Pays for all other platform costs (~$15K-$20K total)
- The rest is PleoChrome's gross profit (~$170K on a $10M deal)

**Key insight:** PleoChrome collects revenue BEFORE spending money. The setup fee covers costs with margin. This is the cash flow advantage of the orchestration model.

---

### MONEY EVENT 2: Pass-Through Costs (Days 14-70)

**What happens:** The stone owner pays for all certification, appraisal, custody, and insurance costs directly.

```
Stone Owner → $5,000-$10,000 → GIA/SSEF Labs (certification)
Stone Owner → $22,500 → 3 Independent Appraisers ($7,500 each)
Stone Owner → $3,000-$5,000 → Transit Insurance
Stone Owner → $10,000 → Vault Custody (Year 1 at 0.10%)
Stone Owner → $10,000 → Specie Insurance (Year 1 at 0.10%)
Stone Owner → $2,000 → Photography/Media

Total pass-through: approximately $52,500-$60,000
```

**When:** Throughout Phases 2-3, as each service is needed.

**Why PleoChrome doesn't pay these:** These are costs associated with the STONE, not the platform. If PleoChrome paid them, we'd need to raise them in the offering price, which dilutes investor value. By having the owner pay directly, we keep the offering clean — every dollar raised goes toward the stone's tokenized value, not PleoChrome's overhead.

**The owner's perspective:** "I'm spending ~$60K to unlock $8.7M+ in liquid proceeds from a $10M illiquid asset. That's 0.6% of the stone's value — and I get most of it back in net proceeds."

---

### MONEY EVENT 3: The 3-Appraisal Rule (Day 56)

**What happens:** The three appraisals come back. We average the two lowest to determine the offering value.

```
Example:
Appraisal 1: $9,200,000
Appraisal 2: $9,800,000
Appraisal 3: $10,500,000

Two lowest: $9,200,000 + $9,800,000 = $19,000,000
Average: $19,000,000 / 2 = $9,500,000

OFFERING VALUE: $9,500,000
```

**No money changes hands here** — but this number determines EVERYTHING that follows:
- How many tokens are created ($9.5M ÷ $100K = 95 tokens)
- What the success fee will be ($9.5M × 1.5% = $142,500)
- What the BD placement fee will be ($9.5M × 7% = $665,000)
- What the annual admin fee will be ($9.5M × 0.75% = $71,250/year)

**The stone owner's reaction might be:** "But I think it's worth $10M!" And the answer is: "Two independent experts disagree. The conservative value protects your future investors. If the stone appreciates (emeralds average 12-15% annually), that value will be reflected in future reappraisals."

---

### MONEY EVENT 4: SPV Bank Account (Day 45)

**What happens:** The Series LLC (SPV) opens its own bank account. This is where investor money will flow.

```
PleoChrome Holdings LLC
└── Series A: Emerald Barrel
    └── Bank Account: [Wells Fargo/Chase/etc.]
        This account is SEPARATE from PleoChrome's operating account.
        PleoChrome cannot access this money.
```

**Why a separate account?** If investor money went into PleoChrome's general account, it would be commingled — mixed with PleoChrome's operating funds. That's:
- Illegal under securities law
- A huge red flag for investors
- A liability if PleoChrome ever had financial trouble

The SPV's bank account is a legal firewall. Investor money goes in, offering expenses come out, and the remainder goes to the stone owner.

---

### MONEY EVENT 5: The Token Sale (Days 90-120)

**What happens:** Accredited investors purchase tokens. Their money flows into the SPV bank account.

```
Investor A → $500,000 → SPV Bank Account (buys 5 tokens)
Investor B → $300,000 → SPV Bank Account (buys 3 tokens)
Investor C → $1,000,000 → SPV Bank Account (buys 10 tokens)
... (more investors)

Total raised: $9,500,000 (95 tokens sold at $100,000 each)
```

**How investors pay:** Wire transfer to the SPV bank account. NOT to PleoChrome's account. NOT in crypto. Traditional wire transfer.

**What they get in return:** After their wire is confirmed, PleoChrome (via Brickken) mints tokens to their verified wallet address. They now hold digital tokens that represent legal ownership of a share of the emerald barrel.

**Important:** The token IS NOT a cryptocurrency investment. It's a digital representation of a legal ownership stake in an SPV that holds physical gemstones. The distinction matters for legal and tax purposes.

---

### MONEY EVENT 6: Distribution of Proceeds (Day 120)

**What happens:** The SPV distributes the offering proceeds according to the PPM terms.

```
Total Raised: $9,500,000

DEDUCTIONS:
- BD Placement Fee (7%):        - $665,000  → Broker-Dealer
- PleoChrome Success Fee (1.5%): - $142,500  → PleoChrome
- PleoChrome Setup Fee (2%):     Already paid (Event 1)
                                  ─────────
NET TO STONE OWNER:              $8,692,500  → Stone Owner

The stone owner also already paid: ~$60,000 in pass-through costs (Event 2)

TOTAL ECONOMIC RESULT FOR STONE OWNER:
Started with: $10M illiquid asset
Received: $8,692,500 in liquid cash
Paid: $200,000 setup fee + $60,000 pass-through = $260,000 in costs
Net net: $8,432,500 (84.3% of claimed value) — in liquid cash, from an asset that was previously illiquid
```

**If the stone owner chose NOT to use a broker-dealer** (direct placement under 506(c)):
```
No BD fee: + $665,000
Net to owner: $9,357,500 (93.6% of claimed value)
```

This is why direct placement is so attractive for the first offering.

---

### MONEY EVENT 7: Ongoing Revenue (Year 2+)

**What happens:** PleoChrome earns recurring fees for managing the tokenized asset.

```
ANNUALLY:
PleoChrome Admin Fee:     $71,250/year (0.75% of $9.5M)
Valuation Refresh Fee:    $25,000/year (coordination of annual reappraisal)
Secondary Transfer Fees:  ~$24,000/year (0.25% per transfer, est. 20% turnover)

TOTAL ANNUAL RECURRING:   ~$120,250/year per stone

AT SCALE (20 stones, Year 5):
20 × $120,250 = $2,405,000/year in recurring revenue
```

**This is what investors in PleoChrome (as a company) care about.** Setup and success fees are one-time. Admin fees are recurring. More stones = more recurring revenue. This is the SaaS-like economics that make PleoChrome a scalable business.
