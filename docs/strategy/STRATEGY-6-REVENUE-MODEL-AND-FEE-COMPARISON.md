## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

PleoChrome has three distinct paths to generate revenue from the same underlying gemstone assets. This document models each path independently on a hypothetical $10M gemstone asset, then shows what happens when paths are stacked on the same asset. The numbers demonstrate that **Path B (Tokenization) is the highest-margin standalone path**, but **stacking Path B + Path C (Tokenization + Debt Instruments) produces the highest total lifetime revenue per asset** with acceptable incremental risk.

| Path | Year 1 Revenue | Year 5 Cumulative | Gross Margin | Scalability |
|------|---------------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **A: Fractional Securities** | $425,000 | $925,000 | 48-55% | Moderate |
| **B: Tokenization** | $425,000 | $925,000 | 55-65% | High |
| **C: Debt Instruments** | $300,000 | $1,500,000 | 40-50% | Moderate |
| **B+C Stacked** | $575,000 | $2,075,000 | 50-60% | High |
| **A+B+C Full Stack** | $675,000 | $2,475,000 | 45-55% | Moderate |

### PATH C: DEBT INSTRUMENTS (Asset-Backed Lending Against Gemstone Collateral)

This path treats the gemstone as collateral for loans. PleoChrome either (a) originates loans itself using a lending pool/credit facility, (b) acts as an intermediary connecting borrowers and institutional lenders for a fee, or (c) lends against tokenized gemstone positions (token-backed lending, available once Path B is operational).

**Model: PleoChrome as Lending Intermediary + Participant**

| **Renewal Fee** | 1.0% on refinanced amount (assume 50% renew at Year 2, Year 4) | -- | $30,000 | -- | $30,000 | -- | $60,000 |
| **Workout / Default Fees** | 3.0% on defaulted amount (assume 5% probability, $6M exposure) | -- | -- | $9,000 | -- | -- | $9,000 |

**Debt Instrument Assumptions:**
- Loan-to-value ratio: 60% ($6M loan against $10M gemstone collateral) -- conservative for luxury assets where industry standard is 50-75%
- Interest rate charged to borrower: 12-18% APR (luxury asset lending market rate per Borro/Vasco/Diamond Banc benchmarks)
- PleoChrome's cost of capital (lending pool): 8-10% (institutional credit facility or investor pool)
- Net interest spread to PleoChrome: ~3% on the $2M PleoChrome co-lends
- Servicing fee: 1.0% annually on outstanding balance for managing payments, custody verification, insurance monitoring
- Renewal cycle: 1-2 year loan terms with refinancing option
- Workout fees only collected in actual default scenarios -- modeled at expected value (5% probability x $6M x 3% fee)

The real power of PleoChrome's model is that the SAME physical gemstone can generate revenue through MULTIPLE paths simultaneously. The assets do not compete for capacity -- they compound.

### Stack 1: Tokenize + Lend Against Tokens (Path B + Path C)

1. PleoChrome tokenizes a $10M gemstone collection (Path B) -- earning setup, success, and admin fees
2. Token holders can borrow against their token positions (Path C) -- PleoChrome earns origination and servicing fees
3. The gemstone remains in vault custody throughout -- Chainlink PoR confirms custody regardless of lending activity
4. Token-backed loans use the ERC-3643 tokens as collateral (locked in escrow smart contract)

### Stack 2: Fractional Securities + Secondary Trading (Path A + ATS)

**How it works:**
1. PleoChrome issues traditional fractional securities (Path A)
2. Lists the securities on an ATS (tZERO, PPEX) for secondary trading
3. Earns transaction fees on secondary trades

This stack is essentially Path A upgraded with liquidity -- which effectively becomes a subset of Path B without the blockchain-native infrastructure.

| Revenue Line | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 | Year 4 | Year 5 | 5-Year Total |

**Incremental Cost:** ATS listing fee ($25,000-$50,000 one-time) + annual ATS maintenance ($10,000-$25,000/year)

**Verdict:** Marginal improvement over Path A standalone. The ATS listing adds $22,500 in revenue over 5 years against $75,000-$175,000 in costs. **Not worth it** unless you are already on Path B (which includes secondary trading natively).

### Stack 3: All Three Paths on the Same Asset (A + B + C)

**How it works:**
1. Tokenize the asset (Path B) -- this subsumes Path A because tokenized securities ARE fractional securities
2. List tokens on ATS for secondary trading (included in Path B)
3. Offer token-backed lending to token holders (Path C)
4. Additionally offer asset-backed lending to the original asset holder against the unsold portion

In practice, "all three paths" means **Path B + Path C** because Path B is a superset of Path A. Running Path A separately alongside Path B would be duplicative and legally problematic (two different securities against the same asset).

### 6.4 The Pricing Moat

PleoChrome's fee structure is the lowest in the industry by a wide margin. This is not a race to the bottom -- it is a deliberate competitive advantage:

1. **PleoChrome does not buy assets** (unlike Masterworks), so it does not need to charge 10%+ to cover acquisition capital costs
2. **PleoChrome does not run an exchange** (unlike tZERO), so it does not need 5-7% primary fees to cover exchange infrastructure
3. **PleoChrome's costs are predominantly fixed**, so each additional asset dramatically improves margins
4. **The 0.75% annual admin fee creates predictable recurring revenue** that, at $100M+ AUM, covers all operating costs independently
5. **No profit share / carry** means PleoChrome's interests are aligned with asset holders and investors -- revenue comes from growing AUM, not from extracting exit profits

This positions PleoChrome as the **"Vanguard of alternative assets"** -- win on cost, win on transparency, win on trust, and let the compound effect of growing AUM drive profitability.
