## 1. REVENUE MODEL DEEP DIVE

### 1.1 Fee Structure Analysis

PleoChrome's three-tier fee model:

| Fee | Rate | When Collected | Applied To |
|-----|------|----------------|------------|
| **Setup Fee** | 2.0% of offering value | Upon engagement / first close | Appraised offering value |
| **Success Fee** | 1.5% of capital raised | At each investor close | Actual capital raised |
| **Annual Admin Fee** | 0.75% of AUM | Annually, beginning Month 13 | Total managed asset value |

**How This Compares to Industry:**

| Platform/Model | Setup/Mgmt Fee | Success/Performance Fee | Annual/Ongoing | Notes |
|----------------|---------------|------------------------|----------------|-------|
| **PleoChrome** | 2.0% setup | 1.5% success | 0.75% annual admin | Lower total cost than most comparables |
| **Masterworks** (art) | 10-11% syndication cost | 20% of profits at exit | 1.5% annual mgmt | Masterworks charges significantly more |
| **Securitize** | Custom enterprise | Varies | Platform + TA SaaS fees | Institutional pricing, opaque |
| **Traditional PE Fund** | 0% setup | 20% carry (over hurdle) | 2.0% annual mgmt | "2 and 20" standard |
| **Hedge Funds** | 0% setup | 15-20% performance | 1.5-2.0% annual mgmt | Declining but still standard |
| **Real Estate Syndication** | 1-3% acquisition fee | 20-30% promote/carry | 1-2% asset mgmt | Closest structural comparison |
| **Dalmore BD of Record** | $5K-55K flat setup | 1-3% of raise | N/A | BD costs layered on top |

**Assessment:** PleoChrome's 2% + 1.5% + 0.75% structure is **competitive and defensible**. The combined upfront load (3.5% on a fully subscribed offering) is well below Masterworks' 10-11% and below most real estate syndication models. The 0.75% annual admin is half the standard "2 and 20" management fee. This pricing creates room to either hold firm (the value proposition is strong) or adjust strategically as competitive pressure matures.

## 2. ASSET ACQUISITION STRATEGY

### 2.1 Why Asset Acquisition Is the Bottleneck

PleoChrome's marketplace has two sides: asset holders and investors. But asset acquisition is the **constraining factor** because:

1. Every stone requires 10-14 weeks of processing (GIA, appraisals, legal, custody, tokenization)
2. Each stone requires its own PPM, SPV, legal documents ($15K-$50K incremental legal cost)
3. Vault custody scales with AUM (0.5-1% of value annually)
4. The 3-appraisal rule cannot be parallelized -- it is sequential
5. Each stone consumes founder time: Shane for BD/investor relations, David for operations, Chris for compliance

**Implication:** PleoChrome cannot process more than 2-3 stones simultaneously until it hires dedicated operations staff. Asset acquisition should run ahead of processing capacity by 6-12 months to maintain a healthy pipeline.

### 2.5 Asset Qualification Criteria

Every potential asset must pass these filters before PleoChrome invests time in the 7-Gate process:

#### MANDATORY (Disqualify if Any Fails)

| Criterion | Minimum Standard | Why |
|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| **Minimum appraised value** | $2,000,000 | Below this, fees do not justify the operational overhead ($15K-$50K per-stone legal costs) |
| **GIA certifiable** | Must be gradeable by GIA or equivalent (SSEF, Gubelin) | Without lab certification, valuation is unreliable |
| **Clean provenance** | Documented chain of custody from mine to current holder | Provenance gaps kill investor confidence and create legal liability |
| **OFAC/sanctions clear** | All persons in chain must pass sanctions screening | Federal crime to transact with sanctioned persons |
| **Legal title** | Clear, unencumbered ownership documented | Title disputes make tokenization impossible |
| **No conflict zone sourcing** | Must comply with OECD Due Diligence Guidance | Reputational and legal risk |
| **Holder cooperation** | Asset holder must agree to full KYC/KYB, provenance documentation, 3-appraisal process | If holder resists transparency, walk away |

#### PREFERRED (Strengthen the Deal)

| Criterion | Preferred Standard | Impact |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Existing GIA reports** | Current (within 5 years) GIA Identification & Origin Reports | Saves $5K-$50K in GIA costs, accelerates timeline by 2-3 weeks |
| **Known origin** | Geographic origin confirmed (Colombian, Zambian, Burmese, etc.) | Origin significantly impacts value; Colombian emeralds command 20-40% premium |
| **Single-owner history** | Fewer hands in chain of custody | Simpler provenance documentation |
| **Value concentration** | 3-5 key stones comprise 60%+ of total value | Easier to appraise and market than 500 small stones |
| **Stone type** | Emerald, ruby, sapphire, alexandrite | Deepest collector/investor market; most GIA data available |
| **No treatment** | Untreated or minimally treated stones | Treatment status heavily impacts value; "no treatment" commands premium |

### 6.1 Token Pricing

**Recommended: $10,000 per token**

For a $10M offering, this creates 1,000 tokens. Here is the analysis:

| Token Price | Total Tokens | Minimum Investment (tokens) | Investor Profile | Verdict |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------------|------------------|---------|
| $1,000 | 10,000 | 100 tokens ($100K min) | Feels retail, too many tokens, dilutes exclusivity | Too low |
| $5,000 | 2,000 | 20 tokens ($100K min) | Reasonable, but large token count | Acceptable |
| **$10,000** | **1,000** | **10 tokens ($100K min)** | **Clean numbers, feels substantial, institutional tone** | **Recommended** |
| $25,000 | 400 | 4 tokens ($100K min) | Fewer tokens, higher exclusivity | Alternative |
| $50,000 | 200 | 2 tokens ($100K min) | Very exclusive, limits divisibility | Acceptable for large offerings |
| $100,000 | 100 | 1 token ($100K min) | Extremely exclusive, but one-token minimum is awkward | Too high |

**Why $10,000:** It is a round number that signals seriousness without being exclusionary. It creates enough tokens (1,000 on a $10M offering) for meaningful liquidity on secondary markets while keeping the total supply manageable. Most importantly, it allows investors to buy in increments: 10 tokens ($100K), 25 tokens ($250K), or 100 tokens ($1M).

### 6.2 Minimum Investment Size

**Recommended: $200,000 minimum investment**

**Strategic rationale:**

The March 2025 SEC no-action letter is the key. When the minimum investment is $200,000 or more for natural persons (or $1,000,000 for entities):
- **Self-certification is sufficient** for accredited investor verification
- No need for income verification, tax returns, or third-party letters
- Investor provides a written representation that they are accredited and the investment is not third-party financed
- **This eliminates the most friction-heavy step in the investor onboarding process**

| Minimum | Self-cert eligible? | Verification cost | Friction level | Investor pool impact |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| $50,000 | No | $50-$150/investor | HIGH (document collection) | Larger pool but harder to close |
| $100,000 | No | $50-$150/investor | HIGH | Moderate pool, moderate friction |
| **$200,000** | **Yes** | **$0** | **LOW (self-cert only)** | **Slightly smaller pool, much lower friction** |
| $500,000 | Yes | $0 | LOW | Smaller pool, very low friction |
| $1,000,000 | Yes (entities too) | $0 | LOWEST | Small pool, institutional only |

**The math:** $200K minimum with 50 investors = $10M raised. $200K minimum with 75 investors = $15M raised. These volumes are achievable for a well-positioned Reg D offering.

**Important:** Set the minimum at $200,000 but allow the sales team to accept $100,000 investments with full third-party verification when strategically beneficial (e.g., key relationship, high-profile investor). Document the rationale.

| $12M | $14M | $13M | $12.5M | 10.7% below highest |

### 8.4 When to Raise External Capital

**Recommendation: Do NOT raise external capital in Year 1-2.**

**Rationale:**
1. The first stone's setup fee ($200K) covers startup costs
2. The business reaches operational break-even on the first fully subscribed stone
3. Raising capital pre-revenue gives away equity cheaply
4. The three founders can self-fund $85K each ($255K total) to cover the pre-revenue gap
5. Premature fundraising distracts from the core mission: completing the first deal

**When external capital makes sense:**

| Scenario | Timing | Amount | Purpose | Structure |
