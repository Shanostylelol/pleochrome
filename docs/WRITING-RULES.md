# PleoChrome Writing Rules — Anti-AI Detection Ruleset

**These rules apply to ALL written content: articles, copy, LinkedIn posts, emails, page text.**

---

## BANNED PHRASES (Never use these)

### Structural Crutches
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to note/consider/remember..."
- "It is worth mentioning..."
- "In today's world/landscape/environment..."
- "In conclusion..." / "In summary..." / "To summarize..."
- "Overall..." (as a paragraph opener)
- "Furthermore..." / "Moreover..." / "Additionally..." (as sentence starters)
- "This is because..."
- "That said..." / "That being said..."
- "At its core..."
- "At the end of the day..." (unless it's Shane's natural speech in a LinkedIn post)

### Contrast Patterns (the AI "pivot" move)
- "It's not X, it's Y" / "This isn't about X. It's about Y."
- "The question is not whether X, but whether Y"
- "X is not the answer. Y is."
- "Not X — but Y" (any negation-then-correction pattern)
- "Less about X and more about Y"
- "This is not X. It is Y." (declarative contrast pair)
- "That is not X. That is Y."
- "You are not X. You are Y." (telling the reader what they are/aren't)
- "None of this means X. It means Y."

### "This is" / "That is" Sentence Starters
- Avoid starting sentences with "This is..." or "That is..." more than once per section
- "This creates..." / "This gives..." / "This allows..." / "This means..." — rewrite to name the actual subject
- "That is a..." / "That is the..." — same rule, name what "that" refers to

### Emphasis Hedging
- "Arguably..."
- "Undeniably..."
- "Interestingly..."
- "Notably..."
- "Crucially..."
- "Importantly..."
- "Significantly..."
- "Remarkably..."

### AI Vocabulary (overused by LLMs)
- "Delve" / "delve into"
- "Landscape" (when describing a market or industry)
- "Ecosystem" (unless specifically about blockchain/DeFi)
- "Leverage" (as a verb meaning "use")
- "Utilize" (just say "use")
- "Facilitate" (just say "help" or "enable")
- "Robust" (unless describing actual engineering)
- "Seamless" / "seamlessly"
- "Comprehensive"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Game-changer" / "game-changing"
- "Paradigm" / "paradigm shift"
- "Navigate" (when describing dealing with something)
- "Unlock" (when describing creating value — unless it's the company tagline context)
- "Harness"
- "Empower"
- "Elevate"
- "Streamline"
- "Optimize"
- "Transformative"
- "Innovative" / "innovation" (unless citing a specific thing)
- "Actionable"
- "Holistic"
- "Synergy"

### Filler Transitions
- "Let's take a closer look"
- "With that in mind..."
- "When it comes to..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "In terms of..."
- "As we look ahead..."
- "Moving forward..."
- "The bottom line is..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "Here's the deal..."

### Formatting Giveaways
- Em dashes (—) — replace with commas, periods, or parentheses
- Triple structure lists ("X, Y, and Z" repeated in every paragraph)
- Every paragraph being the same length
- Bold-then-explain pattern (**Term.** Definition follows.) used more than twice per section
- Numbered lists where a paragraph would work better

---

## REQUIRED PATTERNS (Do these instead)

### Sentence Variation
- Mix short sentences (5-8 words) with medium (12-18) and occasional long (25+)
- Never start 3 consecutive paragraphs with the same structure
- At least one paragraph per section should be 1-2 sentences only
- Use sentence fragments occasionally for emphasis. Like this.

### Specificity Over Abstraction
- Replace "high-value assets" with a specific example: "a 47-carat Paraiba tourmaline" or "a 200-acre mineral rights parcel in the Permian Basin"
- Replace "institutional investors" with "family offices" or "credit funds" or whatever the actual buyer is
- Replace "significant growth" with the actual number
- Never describe something as "important" — show why it matters through consequence

### Tone
- Write like you are explaining to a smart friend over coffee, not presenting to a boardroom
- First person is allowed and encouraged in authored articles
- Contractions are fine (don't, won't, it's, they're)
- Questions to the reader are OK but max 1-2 per article
- Humor and self-awareness are OK in LinkedIn posts, restrained in articles

### Structure
- Vary paragraph lengths: some 1 sentence, some 4-5 sentences
- Section headers should be declarative statements, not generic labels ("The SEC Simplified Verification" not "Regulatory Updates")
- Do not start the conclusion by restating the introduction
- End articles with a forward-looking observation or a practical next step, not a summary

### Citations and Data
- When citing data, give the source inline: "Mafrur's 2025 research found..." not "Research shows..."
- Round numbers are OK ("about $36 billion") but fake precision is not ("$36.247 billion" when the source says ~$36B)
- Never fabricate sources. If you can't cite it, don't claim it.

---

## SELF-CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING

Run through this checklist before any content goes live:

1. Read the first sentence of every paragraph. Do they all start differently?
2. Search for "it's worth" and "it's important" — delete every instance
3. Search for em dashes — replace all of them
4. Are there more than 2 bold-then-explain patterns? Restructure.
5. Does every section have at least one specific example (name, number, place)?
6. Read it out loud. Does it sound like a person talking or a document being read?
7. Is the conclusion different from the introduction? (It must be.)
8. Are paragraphs varying in length, or are they all 3-4 sentences?
