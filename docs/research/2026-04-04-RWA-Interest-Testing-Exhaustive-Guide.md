## 1. Overview: The Four Regulatory Pathways {#1-overview}

Before spending a dollar on legal fees or platform setup, you need to understand which regulatory path matches your asset, your investor base, and your budget. Each path has different rules about what you can say, who you can talk to, and when money can change hands.

| Pathway | Max Raise | Who Can Invest | Can You Advertise? | Can You Test Interest First? | Typical Cost to Launch |
|---------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|----------------------------|----------------------|
| **Reg D 506(c)** | Unlimited | Accredited only | Yes (general solicitation allowed) | Yes, but no formal "TTW" provision — advertising IS the test | $80K–$150K |
| **Reg D 506(b)** | Unlimited | Accredited + up to 35 sophisticated | No (pre-existing relationships only) | Only through private conversations | $50K–$100K |
| **Reg A+ Tier 2** | $75M per year | Anyone (retail + accredited) | Yes | Yes — formal "Testing the Waters" provision BEFORE filing | $150K–$350K |
| **Reg CF** | $5M per year | Anyone (with investment limits) | Yes | Yes — formal TTW provision BEFORE filing Form C | $20K–$60K |

**For PleoChrome's use case (high-value assets, likely $1M+ offerings, accredited investor target):**
- **Primary path: Reg D 506(c)** — unlimited raise, general solicitation, accredited only
- **Interest testing path: Reg A+ TTW or Reg CF TTW** — formal testing the waters before committing to full offering
- **Hybrid approach: Start with Reg CF TTW to test demand, then file Reg D 506(c) for the actual raise**

## 2. Pathway 1: Reg D 506(c) — Accredited Investors Only {#2-reg-d}

### What It Is
Rule 506(c) of Regulation D allows you to raise an unlimited amount of capital from verified accredited investors. You can use general solicitation (public advertising, social media, events) to market the offering. The catch: every single investor must be verified as accredited through a reasonable process.

### How to Test Interest Under 506(c)

There is no formal "testing the waters" provision for Reg D. However, because 506(c) allows general solicitation, you can effectively test interest by marketing before the offering is fully ready. The key distinction: you cannot accept money until your offering documents are complete.

**What you CAN do before the offering is live:**
1. Build a landing page describing the investment thesis (not the specific offering)
2. Run digital ads targeting accredited investor demographics
3. Collect email addresses and expressions of interest (not commitments)
4. Host webinars and educational events about the asset class
5. Publish thought leadership content (articles, podcasts, social media)
6. Attend and present at investment conferences

**What you CANNOT do:**
- Accept money or binding commitments
- Quote specific returns or guarantees
- Misrepresent the offering as available when it is not yet filed
- Skip accredited investor verification when the offering goes live

### Step-by-Step Process

**Phase 1: Pre-Offering Interest Gauging (2-4 weeks)**
1. Create a professional landing page with asset thesis, team bios, and "Register Your Interest" form
2. Set up a CRM to track leads (PleoChrome Powerhouse handles this)
3. Launch targeted LinkedIn ads to accredited investor demographics
4. Publish 2-3 educational articles about the asset class
5. Host one webinar explaining the market opportunity (educational, not solicitation)
6. Track: email signups, webinar attendees, inbound inquiries

**Phase 2: Legal Preparation (4-8 weeks, concurrent with Phase 1)**
1. Engage securities counsel to draft PPM, operating agreement, subscription agreement
2. Form SPV (Delaware LLC recommended)
3. Complete asset appraisals and certification (GIA for gemstones)
4. Arrange institutional custody (Brink's, Malca-Amit)
5. Engage a broker-dealer (Dalmore, North Capital, or Rialto)
6. Set up investor verification system (Accredify, VerifyInvestor, or third-party service)

**Phase 3: Offering Launch (ongoing)**
1. File Form D with SEC within 15 days of first sale
2. File blue sky notices in applicable states
3. Begin accepting subscriptions from verified accredited investors
4. Continue marketing under general solicitation rules

### SEC March 2025 Guidance — Game Changer

On March 12, 2025, the SEC issued a No-Action Letter that significantly eased verification requirements for 506(c) offerings. The new guidance allows issuers to satisfy accredited investor verification through investor self-certification when the minimum investment meets certain thresholds:
- **$200,000 minimum** for individual investors
- **$1,000,000 minimum** for entity investors

This means if your minimum investment is $200K or above, you no longer need to collect tax returns, bank statements, or third-party verification letters. A written self-certification from the investor is sufficient.

**Source:** Gibson Dunn, "SEC Provides Bright-Line Test for Investor Verification Under Rule 506(c)" — https://www.gibsondunn.com/sec-provides-bright-line-test-for-investor-verification-under-rule-506c/

## 3. Pathway 2: Reg A+ — Testing the Waters Before Filing {#3-reg-a}

### What It Is
Regulation A+ is sometimes called a "mini-IPO." It allows companies to raise up to $75 million per year from ANYONE (not just accredited investors). The key advantage for interest testing: Reg A+ has an explicit "Testing the Waters" (TTW) provision that lets you gauge interest BEFORE filing with the SEC.

### How Testing the Waters Works

Under Reg A+ Rule 255, you can communicate with potential investors to determine whether there is interest in your offering BEFORE you file your Form 1-A with the SEC. This is unique to Reg A+ and Reg CF — Reg D does not have a formal TTW provision.

**During TTW, you can:**
1. Create marketing materials describing the proposed offering
2. Distribute those materials publicly (ads, social media, email, events)
3. Collect non-binding indications of interest
4. Gauge demand at specific price points and investment minimums

**During TTW, you CANNOT:**
1. Accept money or binding commitments
2. Accept subscriptions or process payments
3. Fail to include required disclaimers in all TTW materials

**Required disclaimer language for all TTW materials:**
> "No money or other consideration is being solicited, and if sent in response, will not be accepted. No offer to buy the securities can be accepted and no part of the purchase price can be received until the offering statement filed by the company with the SEC has been qualified, and any such offer may be withdrawn or revoked, without obligation or commitment of any kind, at any time before notice of acceptance given after the date of qualification."

### Step-by-Step for Reg A+ TTW

1. **Decide on Reg A+ Tier** (Tier 1: up to $20M; Tier 2: up to $75M)
2. **Create TTW materials** — landing page, pitch deck, one-pager (all with required disclaimers)
3. **Launch TTW campaign** — ads, email, social, webinars
4. **Collect interest** — email signups, soft commitment amounts, survey responses
5. **Evaluate results** — if sufficient interest, proceed to filing. If not, pivot or abandon.
6. **If proceeding:** Engage counsel to prepare Form 1-A filing, audited financials (Tier 2), and offering circular
7. **SEC qualification** — SEC reviews Form 1-A (typically 2-6 months)
8. **Offering launch** — once qualified, begin accepting investments from the public

### Costs

| Item | Cost Range |
|------|-----------|
| TTW campaign (landing page, ads, materials) | $5K–$20K |
| Securities counsel (Form 1-A preparation) | $50K–$150K |
| Audited financials (Tier 2 required) | $15K–$40K |
| Transfer agent | $5K–$15K/year |
| Broker-dealer (if used) | 1–7% of capital raised |
| Platform fees (Republic, StartEngine, etc.) | 6–7.5% of capital raised |
| SEC filing and blue sky fees | $5K–$15K |
| **Total to reach offering launch** | **$150K–$350K** |
| **TTW-only cost (testing before committing)** | **$5K–$20K** |

### When Reg A+ Makes Sense for PleoChrome

Reg A+ is worth considering if:
- You want to include non-accredited (retail) investors
- You want to formally test interest before spending $150K+ on legal and compliance
- The offering size justifies the higher upfront cost (at least $5M raise)
- You want ongoing reporting obligations (Tier 2 requires annual and semi-annual reports)

## 4. Pathway 3: Reg CF — Crowdfunding with Testing the Waters {#4-reg-cf}

### What It Is
Regulation CF (Regulation Crowdfunding) allows companies to raise up to $5 million per year from anyone. Like Reg A+, it has a formal TTW provision. The key advantage: much lower cost than Reg A+.

### TTW Under Reg CF

Under Rule 206, before filing Form C, you can solicit interest from the general public, orally or in writing, as long as your materials include these statements:
1. No money is being solicited and will not be accepted
2. No offer to buy can be accepted until the exemption requirements are met
3. A person's indication of interest involves no obligation

### Step-by-Step for Reg CF TTW

1. **Create TTW materials** with required disclaimers
2. **Launch campaign** — can use any channel (social, ads, email, website)
3. **Collect non-binding interest** — track names, amounts, engagement
4. **Evaluate demand** — typically 21-90 day campaign
5. **If proceeding:** File Form C with SEC, engage a FINRA-registered funding portal or broker-dealer
6. **Offering goes live** — minimum 21-day campaign period

### Costs

| Item | Cost Range |
|------|-----------|
| TTW campaign | $2K–$10K |
| Securities counsel (Form C, disclosures) | $10K–$25K |
| Funding portal fees | 5–7.5% of capital raised |
| Transfer agent | $3K–$8K/year |
| **Total to reach offering launch** | **$20K–$60K** |
| **TTW-only cost** | **$2K–$10K** |

### Limitations for PleoChrome
- $5M cap may be too small for high-value asset offerings
- Individual investment limits apply for non-accredited investors
- Must use a FINRA-registered funding portal or broker-dealer
- Requires CPA-reviewed financials (or audited above $1.235M raised)

### Best Use: As a Demand Validation Tool
Even if Reg CF is not the final offering structure, the TTW provision can serve as an inexpensive demand validation tool. Run a Reg CF TTW campaign to collect interest data, then use those results to inform a Reg D 506(c) offering for the actual capital raise.

## 5. Pathway 4: Informal Interest Gauging (Pre-Regulatory) {#5-informal}

Before engaging any regulatory pathway, you can gauge interest through purely educational activities that do not constitute an offer or solicitation of securities.

### What You Can Do Without Any Filing

1. **Publish research and educational content** about the asset class (PleoChrome Knowledge Hub)
2. **Build an email newsletter** with industry insights and market analysis
3. **Host educational webinars** about RWA investment trends
4. **Speak at industry conferences** about the asset class opportunity
5. **Conduct market research surveys** asking about investment preferences
6. **Create a "Coming Soon" or "Learn More" page** that collects interest without describing a specific offering

### Key Rules
- Do not describe a specific offering (security, price, terms)
- Do not accept money or commitments
- Do not promise returns
- Focus on education and industry knowledge, not solicitation

### Metrics to Track
- Email list growth rate
- Webinar attendance and engagement
- Inbound inquiry volume
- Survey response rates and allocation preferences
- Website traffic to educational content
- Social media engagement on industry posts

## 9. Step-by-Step Execution Plan for PleoChrome {#9-execution}

### Recommended Approach: Phased Interest Validation

**Month 1: Educational Foundation (Cost: $5K)**
1. Publish 3-5 Knowledge Hub articles (already done for value creation models)
2. Create an "Investment Interest" landing page on pleochrome.com — NOT an offering, just educational content with "Register to Learn More" form
3. Set up email automation (Resend or Mailchimp)
4. Begin LinkedIn content program (2-3 posts/week)
5. Track: signups, page views, engagement

**Month 2: Targeted Outreach (Cost: $5K–$10K)**
1. Run LinkedIn ads targeting: family offices, accredited investors, alternative asset allocators, gemstone collectors, real estate investors
2. Host first educational webinar: "Understanding RWA Value Creation Models"
3. Begin direct outreach to known industry contacts
4. Attend one relevant conference or networking event
5. Track: webinar registrations, ad click-through rates, direct conversation outcomes

**Month 3: Interest Evaluation (Cost: $2K)**
1. Analyze data: how many people expressed interest? At what amounts? What asset types?
2. Survey interested parties: investment amount range, preferred structure (token vs. fractional vs. debt), timeline
3. Decision point: is there sufficient interest to justify legal spend?
4. If yes: proceed to legal engagement. If no: adjust asset selection, pricing, or target market and repeat.

**Month 4-5: Legal and Compliance Setup (Cost: $40K–$80K)**
1. Engage securities counsel (Bull Blockchain Law or equivalent)
2. Form SPV
3. Draft PPM and offering documents
4. Engage broker-dealer (Dalmore or North Capital recommended)
5. Set up investor verification
6. Complete asset appraisals and custody arrangements

**Month 6: Offering Launch**
1. File Form D
2. Open offering to verified accredited investors
3. Continue marketing under 506(c) general solicitation
4. Track: subscriptions, close rate, time to close
