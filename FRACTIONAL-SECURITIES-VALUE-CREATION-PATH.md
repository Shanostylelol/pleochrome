## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: WHY FRACTIONAL SECURITIES

### The Opportunity

PleoChrome's current model targets accredited investors with minimum investments of $100K-$250K for tokenized gemstone-backed ERC-3643 securities under Reg D 506(c). This limits the addressable investor pool to approximately 13% of US households (accredited investors) and further narrows it to those willing to commit six-figure minimums to an alternative asset class.

Fractional securities -- offering smaller ownership units (as low as $500-$10,000) in gemstone-backed SPVs -- dramatically expand PleoChrome's addressable market in three ways:

1. **Lower minimums under Reg D 506(c):** Reduce the minimum investment to $10,000-$25,000 while remaining accredited-only, expanding the pool of willing investors by 5-10x.

2. **Non-accredited investors under Reg A+ (Tier 2):** Open offerings to all US investors (not just accredited) with minimums as low as $500-$1,000, up to $75M per year per issuer. This follows the Masterworks/Rally model.

3. **Micro-investments under Reg CF:** Enable investments as low as $100-$500 through registered funding portals, up to $5M per year per issuer. Best for building community and brand awareness.

### Why Fractional Works for Gemstones

| Factor | Full Token ($100K+ min) | Fractional ($500-$25K min) |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Investor pool size | ~2,000-5,000 realistic prospects | ~200,000-500,000+ realistic prospects |
| Sales cycle | 2-6 weeks per investor | 1-7 days per investor |
| Revenue per investor | $1,500-$3,750 (success fee) | $7.50-$375 (success fee) |
| Number of investors needed for $10M | 20-100 | 400-20,000 |
| Marketing cost per investor | $2,500-$10,000 | $50-$500 |
| Community/brand building | Minimal (small investor base) | Massive (thousands of stakeholders become ambassadors) |
| Regulatory complexity | Lower (Reg D, no SEC review) | Higher (Reg A+ requires SEC qualification) |
| Ongoing reporting burden | Minimal | Significant (Reg A+ annual/semi-annual reports) |

### The Bottom Line

Fractional securities are not a replacement for PleoChrome's Reg D 506(c) path -- they are a **parallel value creation path** that can operate simultaneously, targeting different investor segments with different gemstone offerings. A $50M emerald barrel could be offered under Reg D to institutional/HNW investors, while a $3M sapphire collection could be offered under Reg A+ or Reg CF to build community and brand presence.

## 2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK COMPARISON

### Reg D 506(c) vs Reg A+ (Tier 2) vs Reg CF: Complete Comparison

| Dimension | Reg D 506(c) | Reg A+ (Tier 2) | Reg CF |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Maximum raise** | Unlimited | $75M per 12-month period | $5M per 12-month period |
| **Investor eligibility** | Accredited investors ONLY | All investors (accredited + non-accredited) | All investors (accredited + non-accredited) |
| **Non-accredited investor limits** | Not permitted | 10% of annual income or net worth per investor (non-accredited) | 10% of annual income or net worth per investor (if either < $124K); if both >= $124K, 10% of greater |
| **Minimum investment** | Set by issuer (typically $25K-$250K for gemstones) | Set by issuer (can be as low as $1; Masterworks uses $500-$1,000) | Set by issuer (can be as low as $100) |
| **General solicitation** | YES (permitted) | YES (permitted) | YES (via registered portal) |
| **SEC review/qualification** | NO (notice filing only) | YES -- SEC must "qualify" the offering (2-6 months) | YES -- SEC reviews Form C (2-4 weeks) |
| **Primary disclosure document** | PPM (Private Placement Memorandum) | Offering Circular (Form 1-A, Parts I-III) | Form C (offering statement) |
| **Ongoing SEC reporting** | Annual Form D amendment only | Annual report (Form 1-K), semi-annual report (Form 1-SA), current reports (Form 1-U) | Annual report (Form C-AR) |
| **Audited financials required** | No (recommended but not required) | YES -- 2 years audited financials (Tier 2) | Yes, if raising > $618K (2026 threshold); reviewed financials if raising $124K-$618K |
| **Transfer restrictions** | Restricted securities -- 6-12 month holding period (Rule 144) | Freely tradeable upon issuance (NOT restricted) | 1-year holding period (transfers to issuer, accredited investors, family, or in estate allowed) |
| **Blue Sky (state) preemption** | YES -- federal preemption under NSMIA | YES (Tier 2 only) -- federal preemption | YES -- federal preemption |
| **Intermediary required** | No (but BD recommended) | No (but BD typically used) | YES -- must use SEC-registered funding portal or broker-dealer |
| **Bad actor disqualification** | YES (Rule 506(d)) | YES (Reg A Rule 262) | YES (Rule 503(a)) |
| **Testing the waters** | N/A (can solicit immediately) | YES -- can solicit interest before filing with SEC | YES -- can solicit interest before filing |
| **Investor verification** | REQUIRED (reasonable steps to verify accredited status) | Not required (non-accredited self-certify) | Not required (self-certification with investment limits) |
| **Timeline to first sale** | 8-16 weeks | 4-12 months (SEC qualification is the bottleneck) | 2-4 months |
| **Upfront cost (legal + filing)** | $50,000-$180,000 | $150,000-$500,000+ | $50,000-$150,000 |
| **Annual compliance cost** | $10,000-$30,000 | $50,000-$150,000+ | $25,000-$75,000 |
| **Securities type** | Restricted securities | Unrestricted (freely tradeable) | Restricted (1-year hold) |

### Which Regulation for Which Gemstone Offering?

| Offering Scenario | Best Regulation | Rationale |
|-------------------|----------------|-----------|
| Single stone, $10M-$50M, targeting HNW/institutional | **Reg D 506(c)** | No SEC review delay, unlimited raise, natural HNW audience |
| Collection, $5M-$30M, building retail brand awareness | **Reg A+ (Tier 2)** | Non-accredited investors, freely tradeable shares, secondary market ready |
| Entry-level stone, $1M-$5M, community building | **Reg CF** | Lowest cost to launch, builds grassroots investor community, funding portal handles compliance |
| International investors (non-US) | **Reg D 506(c) + Reg S** | Reg S exemption for non-US persons, combined with 506(c) for US accredited |
| Maximum flexibility, hybrid approach | **Reg D 506(c) + Reg A+** | Use 506(c) for large institutional checks, Reg A+ for retail distribution of same or different asset |

### Critical Regulatory Notes

**Reg A+ Exclusion Warning:** Regulation A explicitly excludes "fractional undivided interests in oil or gas rights, or similar interests in other mineral rights." Gemstones are NOT mineral rights in the traditional sense (they are finished goods/collectibles, not extraction rights), but this exclusion must be addressed in the offering circular and confirmed by securities counsel. The distinction is critical: PleoChrome is offering membership interests in an LLC that owns gemstones -- NOT direct fractional interests in the gemstones themselves. The LLC wrapper is essential for Reg A+ eligibility.

**Reg CF Portal Requirement:** Under Reg CF, ALL transactions must occur through an SEC-registered funding portal or broker-dealer. PleoChrome cannot sell directly. Portals include Wefunder, Republic, StartEngine, and Dealmaker. The portal takes a percentage (typically 5-10% of raise or flat fees).

## 3. PLATFORM MODEL COMPARISON

### Masterworks Model (Art Fractional Securities)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Asset class** | Blue-chip fine art (Warhol, Basquiat, Banksy, etc.) |
| **Regulation** | Reg A+ (Tier 2) -- each painting filed separately with SEC |
| **Entity structure** | Delaware LLC per painting; Class A shares (investor), Class B shares (management), Preferred shares; Cayman Islands SPC owns the art, LLC owns SPC shares |
| **Minimum investment** | $500-$1,000 (lowered from $15,000-$20,000 over time) |
| **Investor eligibility** | All investors (Reg A+ allows non-accredited) |
| **Fee structure** | 10-11% upfront syndication cost baked into share price + 1.5% annual management fee (via share dilution) + 20% of profits at exit |
| **Secondary market** | Proprietary trading platform (not a registered ATS -- uses bulletin board model) |
| **Transfer agent** | SEC-registered transfer agent maintains shareholder records |
| **Holding period** | 3-10 years (art is sold at auction or private sale) |
| **AUM** | $1B+ across 300+ paintings |
| **Key innovation** | Made art investing accessible to retail investors through Reg A+ + low minimums |
| **Annual reporting** | Annual report (Form 1-K) filed with SEC for each LLC; semi-annual reports (Form 1-SA); current reports (Form 1-U) for material events |
| **Independent appraisals** | Required annually for insurance and SEC compliance |

**PleoChrome Takeaway:** Masterworks proves the model works for physical collectible assets. Their 10-11% upfront load + 1.5% annual + 20% carry is significantly more expensive than PleoChrome's proposed fee structure. However, Masterworks spent heavily on SEC qualification ($150K-$300K per painting for legal and filing costs) and needed massive scale (300+ paintings) to amortize those costs. PleoChrome must evaluate whether the per-asset cost of Reg A+ qualification is justified for individual gemstone offerings.

### Rally Model (Collectibles Fractional Securities)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Asset class** | Cars, watches, wine, art, books, memorabilia, comics |
| **Regulation** | Reg A+ (Tier 2) |
| **Entity structure** | Series LLC per asset |
| **Minimum investment** | As low as $50 per share |
| **Investor eligibility** | All investors |
| **Fee structure** | Markup baked into initial share price (undisclosed percentage); no ongoing management fee; transaction fees on secondary trades |
| **Secondary market** | Proprietary trading platform with scheduled "trading windows" |
| **Transfer agent** | SEC-registered |
| **Holding period** | Indefinite (some assets sold at auction; most held for appreciation) |
| **Regulatory history** | SEC enforcement action -- Rally (RSE) violated Section 5 of the Exchange Act by operating an unregistered national securities exchange. Rally subsequently registered properly. |

**PleoChrome Takeaway:** Rally's enforcement action is a critical lesson -- operating a secondary trading platform without proper ATS or exchange registration is a serious violation. PleoChrome must either use a registered ATS for secondary trading or not offer it at all. Rally's ultra-low minimums ($50) drove massive adoption but thin per-investor revenue.

### STEP 2: REGULATORY PATHWAY SELECTION

**Description:** For each gemstone asset entering the pipeline, PleoChrome selects the optimal SEC exemption (Reg D 506(c), Reg A+ Tier 2, or Reg CF) based on the asset's value, target investor profile, and strategic objectives. This is a per-offering decision, not a one-time choice.

**Who Is Involved:**
- Shane Pierson (CEO) -- strategic decision
- Chris Ramsey (CRO) -- asset holder relationship, deal size input
- Securities attorney -- regulatory counsel
- Broker-dealer (if already engaged) -- distribution capability input

**Legal Gates:**
- Confirm the asset is eligible under chosen exemption (Reg A+ excludes certain mineral interests -- must confirm gemstones are not "similar interests in mineral rights")
- Confirm PleoChrome and all covered persons are eligible under the chosen exemption's bad actor provisions
- For Reg A+: confirm issuer is organized in US or Canada (Wyoming LLC qualifies)
- For Reg A+: confirm issuer is NOT a "blank check company" (PleoChrome has an identified asset/business purpose, so this should not apply)
- For Reg CF: confirm issuer is organized in the US and is not an Exchange Act reporting company

**Cost Gates:**

| Decision Factor | Reg D 506(c) | Reg A+ (Tier 2) | Reg CF |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| Legal/filing cost to launch this offering | $50K-$180K | $150K-$500K | $50K-$150K |
| Ongoing annual compliance cost | $10K-$30K | $50K-$150K | $25K-$75K |
| Time to first sale | 8-16 weeks | 4-12 months | 2-4 months |
| Break-even offering size | ~$1.5M | ~$5M-$10M | ~$1M-$2M |

**Pathway Selection Decision Matrix:**

| Criterion | Weight | Reg D 506(c) Score | Reg A+ Score | Reg CF Score |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Offering size > $10M | 3x | 5 | 4 | 1 |
| Target includes non-accredited | 2x | 1 | 5 | 5 |
| Speed to market critical | 2x | 5 | 1 | 3 |
| Secondary market liquidity desired | 2x | 3 | 5 | 2 |
| Budget-constrained launch | 1x | 4 | 1 | 3 |
| Community/brand building goal | 1x | 1 | 4 | 5 |
| Ongoing reporting tolerance | 1x | 5 | 1 | 2 |

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks (decision and documentation)

**Dependencies:** Step 1 (entity formation) must be complete. Asset must be identified (Step 3 in parallel).

**Revenue for PleoChrome:** None at this step. However, the pathway selection directly determines PleoChrome's fee potential:
- Reg D 506(c): Higher per-investor revenue, lower volume
- Reg A+: Lower per-investor revenue, much higher volume potential, more ongoing compliance revenue
- Reg CF: Lowest per-investor revenue, highest volume, funded primarily by success fees

**Deliverables/Outputs:**
- Regulatory pathway selection memo (signed by CEO and counsel)
- Budget estimate for chosen pathway
- Timeline estimate for chosen pathway
- Preliminary offering structure (number of units, price per unit, minimum investment)

**Risk Factors:**
- Choosing Reg A+ for a small offering ($3M-$5M) may not justify the $150K-$500K qualification cost
- Choosing Reg D for a community-building offering misses the brand-building opportunity
- Regulatory uncertainty: SEC may challenge gemstone SPVs under Reg A+ if they view the offering as "similar interests in mineral rights"
- Selecting the wrong pathway wastes 3-12 months and $50K-$500K if the offering must be restructured

### STEP 3: ASSET ACQUISITION AND VERIFICATION

**Description:** Identify, acquire or engage, and verify the physical gemstone(s) that will underlie the fractional securities offering. This follows PleoChrome's 7-Gate Framework: GIA certification, 3-appraisal consensus, provenance chain, vault custody, insurance, Chainlink Proof of Reserve (if tokenized), and compliance verification.

**Who Is Involved:**
- Chris Ramsey (CRO) -- primary asset sourcer, provenance investigator
- Shane Pierson (CEO) -- deal negotiation, engagement agreement execution
- David Whiting (CTO/COO) -- operational coordination (GIA, vault, insurance)

- Engagement agreement between PleoChrome and asset holder must be executed

**Revenue for PleoChrome:**

- Sanctions screening flags on any party in the chain of custody
- Asset holder becomes uncooperative during verification process
- Vault or insurance costs exceed projections for high-value assets
- Treatment status (heated, oiled, filled) discovered that was not disclosed by asset holder

### STEP 4: SPV FORMATION AND UNIT STRUCTURING

**Description:** Form a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) -- typically a Wyoming Series LLC subsidiary -- that will own the gemstone(s) and issue the fractional ownership interests (units/shares) to investors. Structure the membership units, classes, and economic terms.

**Who Is Involved:**
- Shane Pierson (CEO) -- structural decisions, manager authority
- David Whiting (CTO/COO) -- operational setup, cap table configuration
- Securities attorney -- SPV operating agreement, unit structure design
- Tax attorney/CPA -- tax classification, partnership allocation modeling
- Transfer agent (if using one) -- unit structure compatibility review
- Tokenization platform (Brickken) -- if using blockchain layer, token-to-unit mapping

**Legal Gates:**
- SPV must be organized as a "covered security" under the chosen exemption for federal preemption of state blue sky laws
- Operating agreement must comply with the Securities Act and all applicable state laws
- Unit structure must clearly define: economic rights (distributions, liquidation preference), voting rights (if any), transfer restrictions, and redemption rights (if any)
- If using blockchain tokens to represent units: the token IS the security, and the on-chain record must reconcile with the legal cap table
- SPV operating agreement must include: anti-dilution protections, drag-along/tag-along rights (if applicable), information rights, and exit provisions
- For Reg A+: must create distinct share/unit classes (Masterworks uses Preferred, Class A, Class B)
- For Reg CF: must comply with funding portal requirements for unit structure

**SPV Unit Structuring Options:**

**Option A: Percentage Interest (Simple)**
- Each investor holds a percentage of the LLC
- Pro: Simple, familiar to traditional investors
- Con: Difficult to divide into small fractions, awkward for secondary trading

**Option B: Membership Units (Recommended for Fractional)**
- Issue a fixed number of membership units (e.g., 10,000 units for a $10M offering at $1,000/unit, or 100,000 units at $100/unit)
- Pro: Easily divided, familiar equity-like structure, secondary market compatible
- Con: Requires robust transfer agent and cap table tracking

**Option C: Membership Units + Blockchain Token (Hybrid)**
- Issue membership units that are represented on-chain by ERC-3643 tokens (1:1 mapping)
- Pro: Combines legal clarity with blockchain transparency and programmable compliance
- Con: Highest complexity, requires reconciliation between legal and on-chain records

**Recommended Unit Structure by Regulation:**

| Regulation | Unit Type | Par Value | Total Units (for $10M offering) | Min Investment |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-------------------------------|----------------|
| Reg D 506(c) | Membership units + ERC-3643 tokens | $1,000 | 10,000 | $25,000 (25 units) |
| Reg A+ (Tier 2) | Class A membership interests | $10-$25 | 400,000-1,000,000 | $500-$1,000 |
| Reg CF | Membership units | $100 | 50,000 (for $5M max) | $100-$500 |

**Class Structure (for Reg A+ offerings, following Masterworks model):**

| Class | Holder | Rights |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Class A (Investor Shares)** | Public investors | Pro-rata economic rights, limited voting, no management authority |
| **Class B (Manager Shares)** | PleoChrome Holdings LLC | Sole management authority, 20% carried interest on profits at exit, 1.5% annual management share (via dilution of Class A), voting control |
| **Preferred Shares** | PleoChrome Holdings LLC (or asset holder) | Liquidation preference for setup/acquisition costs before any distribution to Class A |

**Cost Gates:**

| Item | Low | Mid | High |
|------|-----|-----|------|
| SPV LLC formation (Wyoming) | $100 | $200 | $500 |
| Operating agreement (attorney-drafted, complex) | $5,000 | $15,000 | $30,000 |
| Tax opinion / partnership allocation modeling | $3,000 | $8,000 | $20,000 |
| Registered agent (annual) | $50 | $150 | $300 |
| EIN application | $0 | $0 | $0 |
| Cap table setup (platform) | $0 | $5,000 | $20,000 |
| Token deployment (if hybrid, via Brickken) | $0 | $5,000 | $25,000 |
| **Total** | **$8,150** | **$33,350** | **$95,800** |

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks (can run in parallel with Step 3)

**Dependencies:** Step 1 (parent entity), Step 2 (pathway selection), Step 3 (asset value determines unit count and pricing)

**Revenue for PleoChrome:**
- No direct revenue at this step
- The SPV structure directly determines PleoChrome's economic interest: management fees, carried interest, and preferred returns are embedded in the operating agreement
- **Structural revenue embedded in SPV:**
  - 2.0% setup fee (Preferred Share or first-dollar distribution)
  - 1.5% success fee on capital raised (paid at close from offering proceeds)
  - 0.75% annual admin fee (paid from SPV cash reserves or via Class A dilution)
  - 15-20% carried interest on profits at exit (Class B participation)

**Deliverables/Outputs:**
- SPV Certificate of Formation
- SPV Operating Agreement (executed, with all class definitions)

**Legal Gates:**

**For Reg D 506(c):**

**Legal Gates:**
